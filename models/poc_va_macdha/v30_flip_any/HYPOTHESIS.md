# v30_flip_any — Coulling VPA flips (any stock)

## The story (from Anna Coulling VPA)

**Volume = effort. Price spread = result.** They must agree.

When they don’t (anomaly), smart money is absorbing or distributing.

Book in repo:
- `books/Anna Coulling A Complete Guide To Volume Price Analysis ( PDFDrive ).pdf`
- Plain notes: `books/WHAT_THE_BOOKS_SAY.md`

## Patterns we track → flips

| VPA pattern | Meaning | Trade |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| **Stopping volume** | High vol + narrow after a fall — brakes / buying | Watch longs |
| **No supply** after stop | Dip on low vol — test | **CALL** |
| **Stopping reclaim / spring** | Reclaim after absorption | **CALL** |
| **Confirm up** (price↑ vol↑) | Harmony | **CALL** (if strong) |
| **Topping volume** | High vol + narrow into highs — distribution | Watch shorts |
| **No demand** | Rally on low vol — trap | **PUT** / block CALL |
| **Upthrust fail** | Break high fails | **PUT** |
| **Dump** (price↓ vol↑) | Real selling | **PUT** |
| **Buying climax** | Frenzy high vol into highs | **Block chase CALL** |
| **Effort anomaly** | Wide move on thin vol | Weak / skip |

## Style
- **Any stock** in the universe
- Long **call or put only** (no spreads)
- Short hold (1–5d), aggressive size
- Max concurrent names (default 3)

## Why this is the whole story
Price alone is noisy. Coulling’s edge is reading **whether volume validates the bar**.  
That is what we now use to decide call vs put, not bare green/red days.
