## How he thinks (core thesis)

1. **Institutions trade known levels; retail reacts late.** Goal is to mark those levels *before* the move, not chase after retail is in.
2. **Structure first, narrative second.** Start with OHLC, gaps, MAs, trend lines, breadth — then layer news (Meta, Samsung, oil, Fed).
3. **Semis are the tell for Nasdaq risk.** If SOX/semis lose structure, “you don’t have anything” for growth leadership. High relative strength = play the leaders (often semis/memory when healthy), not the weak names.
4. **Rotation ≠ bear market.** SPX/Nasdaq relative, XLY/XLP, XLV, breadth can show money rotating *inside* the market. Don’t confuse sector rotation with “get out of equities.”
5. **Don’t fight rising earnings** in semis when the street keeps raising estimates — skepticism alone is not a thesis.

## What he looks for (checklist)

### Market structure
- **Gap fills** — “old-fashioned gap fill” is desirable: flush weak hands / clean supply, then retest can become support.
- **Island reversal gaps** — gap up / gap down isolating a block of price. Expect **~3–5 days** of resolution pattern; watch top/bottom of the island as hard levels.
- **Higher highs / lower lows on closes** — change of trend is about *closes*, not wicks. Undercut → reclaim (“grab and rip”) is constructive.
- **Downward trend lines (DTL)** — popping above early then rejecting without a retest is a warning; need sustained closes above the break.
- **Fibonacci** — especially **61.8%** and **78.6%** from major swing lows/highs; set alerts on fibs.
- **Value area / prior key bars** — lows of value areas and prior earnings bars as hold-or-fail pivots.

### Moving averages & “the 12 / 22 / 55”
- Watches **12, 22, and 55** constantly (his short/medium structure stack).
- **Close under the 22** on a leader (e.g. Micron / DRAM complex) after a big run = first real warning.
- **55 = institutional support test.** Close under 55 on NDX *and* SOX together = risk-off for semi swings.
- **50-day / NDFI** — NDFI ≈ Nasdaq names above their 50-DMA. Rising NDFI / reclaim = breadth improving; close under 50 on index → avoid new swing longs.

### Relative strength & rotation
- Prefers **buying relative strength** (name/sector vs SPY/QQQ), not catching knives in laggards.
- Ratios he actually uses on video: **SPX/NDX (or SPY/QQQ)**, **XLK/SPY**, **XLY/XLP**, sector vs Nasdaq as demarcation.
- Defensive rotation (XLP/XLV): he often **stands aside / goes cash** — not enough “meat” for his style versus trading Clorox-type names.
- Window dressing / quarter-end: expect fake strength then gap/flush — don’t treat end-of-quarter rip as free trend.

### Options / dealer levels
- Marks **put walls** and **call walls** on SPX/QQQ (examples in this window: ~700 / ~740 put walls; name-level call walls).
- Near put walls: often **does not trail stop to breakeven immediately** — wants room for the bounce thesis.
- When VIX looks calm (~15) but **VXN** is elevated (~mid-20s), under-the-hood Nasdaq vol is the real story — good for **credit / short premium** if you know the levels.
- Uses ATR / expected move so gamma decay isn’t a surprise when realized move shrinks.

### VWAP family
- **IPO VWAP:** if price can’t reclaim IPO VWAP, longs fight every IPO buyer — stand aside or short bias.
- **Event / gap VWAP:** overlay VWAP from a gap-down (or key event) as a trigger level.
- Compare IPO VWAP behavior across “rocket” peers (relationship matters, not one chart in isolation).

### Process / routine
- Walks levels **premarket**, then reviews open → OHLC → sector → single names.
- Prefers **bare OHLC charts** to strip noise.
- Maps **index → sector (SOX/SOXL) → stock** and sizes risk accordingly.
- On trend days: trail stops with structure; move stops up as structure allows.
- Macro catalysts he won’t ignore: Fed path, NFP, bond auctions, oil/geopolitics, mega-cap CapEx (Meta), Asian semi earnings (Samsung, TSMC, SK Hynix).

## Risk & stand-aside rules

- **No swing longs in semis** when bears own the 55 and RS is broken — day-trade/short or wait.
- **Close below key MA stack + lower-low close** = problem; care about the *close*, not the intraday spike.
- **Don’t buy weak defensives** for “safety” if the edge isn’t there — cash is a position.
- **Headline/CapEx shocks** can trap window-dressing longs — wait for structure reclaim.
- Size by instrument: SOXL/single name > index risk; put-wall bounces still need a stop *behind* the level.
- Overnight binary (geopolitics, earnings) → often **skip overnight risk**.

---

## Recurring mantras

- Institutions trade levels ahead of time; don’t get the info after retail is already in.
- Gap fill first — clean supply, then retest.
- If we lose the semis, we don’t have anything.
- Play the high-relative-strength game.
- The 55 answers: do I have institutional support or not?
- If you can’t get above IPO VWAP, you’re fighting every IPO buyer.
- VIX at 15 can lie; check VXN / single-name vol.
- Rotation into beaten-up sectors ≠ rotation out of the market.
- Until earnings go away on semiconductors, they’re not going anywhere (when estimates keep rising).
